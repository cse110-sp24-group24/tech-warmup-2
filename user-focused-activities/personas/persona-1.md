# User Persona 1: The Gambling Addict

**Name:** Marcus Thorne  
**Age:** 34  
**Occupation:** Day Trader / Crypto Enthusiast  

## Background
Marcus is a highly analytical user who treats slot machines and digital betting as a numbers game. He understands concepts like RNG (Random Number Generation), RTP (Return to Player), and volatility. He isn't playing to kill time, he is playing to beat the system and maximize his money. He has a high tolerance for risk and actually prefers high-volatility machines (where he might lose 10 times in a row, but the 11th win is massive). 

## Wants & Needs
* **Speed:** He needs a "Fast Spin" or "Skip Animation" button. He wants to process as many bets per minute as possible.
* **Auto-Play:** A feature to automatically spin 10, 50, or 100 times at a set bet amount.
* **Clear Paytables:** He needs to know exactly what a "Cherry" symbol pays versus a "Number 7" symbol before he starts betting.
* **High Volatility:** He wants the option to place "Max Bets" for a chance at a massive payout, rather than just winning his original bet back constantly.
* **Transparent Mechanics:** He likes seeing his exact token balance update instantly without delay. 

## Doesn't Want / Doesn't Need
* **Long, Unskippable Animations:** He hates when a machine forces him to watch a 10-second animation of coins dropping. It slows down his betting loop.
* **Confusing "Bonus" Games:** He doesn't want to play a mini-game to get his payout. Keep the core loop strictly to the reels.
* **Low Stakes:** He doesn't want a machine that only lets him bet 1 token at a time.
* **"Fluff" and Lore:** He doesn't care about the backstory of the machine theme; he just wants to know the multiplier on the symbols.

## Technical Literacy & Frustrations
* **Literacy:** Very high. If the UI lags or the RNG feels "rigged" instead of truly random, he will immediately notice and stop playing.
* **Frustrations:** Platforms that crash during a spin, hidden fees, or unclear rules on how the wildcard symbol actually works. 

## Goal for our Software
If we are building this for Marcus, our JavaScript math module needs to have 0 mistakes, our UI needs to be snappy without lag, and we *must* include a bet sizing feature (e.g., Bet 10 vs. Bet 100).
