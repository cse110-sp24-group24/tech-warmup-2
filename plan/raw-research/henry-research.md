# Slot Machine Industry Research

## Overall Design / UI
- Slot machines use **bright colors, animations, and clear themes** (casino, treasure, ocean, etc.)  
- The **reels are the main focus** and take up most of the screen  
- A **large spin button or lever** makes interaction simple  
- Important info like **balance, bet, and result** is always visible  
- Too many effects can make it confusing, so balance is important  

## Player Experience
- Spins are designed to feel exciting, not just random  
- Reels usually **stop one at a time** to build suspense  
- Different sounds are used for small wins vs big wins  
- “Near-misses” (almost winning) are often used to keep players engaged  

## Game Types
- **Gambling-focused**: fewer features, designed to make money over time  
- **Casual/fun**: more wins, includes extra features like power-ups  
- **Hybrid**: mixes both with fast gameplay and bonus features  

## Common Features
- Spin button  
- Bet controls  
- Win/loss messages  
- Sound effects  
- Paytable (shows rewards)  
- Bonus rounds or jackpots  

## Technical Basics
- Uses **random number generation (RNG)** for outcomes  
- Needs to be **fast and responsive** (quick spins, smooth animations)  
- Should handle edge cases:
  - No negative balance  
  - Bet cannot exceed balance  

## Key Takeaways
- Simple and clear design works better than overly flashy UI  
- Player engagement comes from **timing, sound, and feedback**  
- The core of every slot machine is **spinning reels + random outcomes**
